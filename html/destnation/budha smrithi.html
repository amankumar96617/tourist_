Know more about the beauty of Budhasmirti

Budhasmirti, Patna

Buddha Smriti Park, also known as Buddha Memorial Park, is a beautifully designed urban park located in the heart of Patna, Bihar, near the Patna Junction railway station. It was inaugurated on May 27, 2010, by the 14th Dalai Lama, to commemorate the 2554th birth anniversary of Lord Buddha.

The park was developed by the Bihar government as a tribute to the life, teachings, and legacy of Gautama Buddha.

Overview of Budhasmriti, Patna

Top Places to Explore

  • Vikramshila University –An ancient center of learning established during the Pala Empire.
  • Takht Sri Patna Sahib –A sacred Sikh shrine marking the birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh.
  • Golghar – Historic granary with city view.
  • Eco Park – Famous for: Green park, boating & relaxing
  • Patliputra Museum – Rich collection of Bihar heritage.

How to Reach Budhasmirti

✈ By Air: The nearest airport is Jay Prakash Narayan International Airport.

🚆 By Rail: Nearest stations are Patliputra Junction and Patna Junction.

🛣 By Road: It is well connected by road within Patna through buses, taxis, and local transport.

Travel Desk

Tourist help desks and local guides are available in Patna city. Visitors can book taxis and local tours from major hotels and travel agencies.

Famous Food of Patna

  • Litti Chokha – Famous traditional Bihari dish.
  • Sattu Paratha – Healthy regional specialty.
  • Khaja – Popular crispy sweet.
  • Chana Ghugni – Spicy street food.
  • Malpua & Rabri – Festive dessert.

Shopping in Patna

Popular shopping areas near Patna include Maurya Lok, Hathwa Market and local street markets where traditional Bihari items are available.

Major Festivals

  • Chhath Puja – Grand celebration at Digha Ghat.
  • Makar Sankranti – Kite flying festival.
  • Holi – Colorful celebrations.
  • Diwali – Festival of lights.
  • Durga Puja – Major pandal celebrations.
